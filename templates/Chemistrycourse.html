Core Concepts

Chemistry is built on several fundamental principles:

  • Atomic Theory: Structure of atoms
  • Periodic Table: Organization of elements
  • Chemical Bonding: Ionic, covalent, metallic
  • States of Matter: Solids, liquids, gases
  • Conservation Laws: Mass and energy

Branches of Chemistry

Major areas of chemical study:

  1. Organic Chemistry: Carbon compounds
  2. Inorganic Chemistry: Non-carbon compounds
  3. Physical Chemistry: Physics of chemical systems
  4. Analytical Chemistry: Composition analysis
  5. Biochemistry: Chemistry of living organisms

Scientific Method

Chemistry's approach to investigation:

  • Observation: Noticing phenomena
  • Hypothesis: Proposed explanation
  • Experiment: Controlled testing
  • Analysis: Data interpretation
  • Conclusion: Theory development

Atomic Structure

The building blocks of matter

Quantum Insight: Atoms are mostly empty space - if an atom were the size of a football stadium, the nucleus would be a marble at center field.

Subatomic Particles

Components of atoms:

  • Protons: Positive charge, nucleus
  • Neutrons: Neutral, nucleus
  • Electrons: Negative charge, orbitals
  • Quarks: Protons/neutrons components
  • Antimatter: Opposite counterparts

Electron Configuration

Arrangement of electrons:

  • Quantum Numbers: n, l, m, s
  • Orbitals: s, p, d, f shapes
  • Pauli Exclusion: No two identical electrons
  • Hund's Rule: Maximize parallel spins
  • Valence Electrons: Outer shell chemistry
1s² 2s² 2p⁶ 3s² 3p⁶ 4s¹ # Potassium

Periodic Trends

Patterns in element properties:

Atomic Properties
  • Atomic Radius: Size of atoms
  • Ionization Energy: Remove electron
  • Electronegativity: Attract electrons
  • Electron Affinity: Gain electron
Group Characteristics
  • Alkali Metals: Group 1
  • Halogens: Group 17
  • Noble Gases: Group 18
  • Transition Metals: d-block

Nuclear Chemistry

Changes in atomic nuclei:

Process Description Example
Radioactive Decay Spontaneous nuclear breakdown Uranium-238 → Thorium-234
Nuclear Fission Heavy nucleus splits Nuclear power plants
Nuclear Fusion Light nuclei combine Sun's energy source

Chemical Bonding

How atoms combine to form molecules

Types of Bonds

Forces holding atoms together:

Ionic
  • Metal + Nonmetal
  • Electron transfer
  • High melting points
  • Example: NaCl
Covalent
  • Nonmetal + Nonmetal
  • Electron sharing
  • Molecular compounds
  • Example: H₂O
Metallic
  • Metal + Metal
  • Electron sea
  • Conductive
  • Example: Cu

Molecular Geometry

VSEPR Theory predictions:

Shapes
  • Linear (180°)
  • Trigonal Planar (120°)
  • Tetrahedral (109.5°)
  • Octahedral (90°)
Polarity
  • Dipole moments
  • Electronegativity differences
  • Intermolecular forces
  • Solubility effects

Intermolecular Forces

Between-molecule attractions:

  • London Dispersion: Temporary dipoles
  • Dipole-Dipole: Polar molecules
  • Hydrogen Bonds: H with N,O,F
  • Ion-Dipole: Ions with polar molecules
  • Effects on boiling points

Types

  • Structural: Atom arrangement

  • C₆H₁₂O₆ (Glucose)

Chemical Reactions

Transformation of substances

Reaction Types

Classification of chemical changes:

  • Combination: A + B → AB
  • Decomposition: AB → A + B
  • Single Replacement: A + BC → AC + B
  • Double Replacement: AB + CD → AD + CB
  • Combustion: Fuel + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O
2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O # Combination

Thermochemistry

Energy in reactions:
